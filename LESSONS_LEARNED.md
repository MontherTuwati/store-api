# Lessons Learned

## 1. I need to simplify my approach when solving problems

During the testing phase, I initially explored more complex solutions before settling on a simple Node.js script that met the requirements. This added unnecessary time and effort, even though the final solution was straightforward.

In future projects, I will focus on understanding the requirement first and choosing the simplest solution that satisfies it, instead of overcomplicating the implementation.

---

## 2. I need to improve how I handle deployment issues

While working with deployed APIs, I ran into issues related to database connectivity and environment configuration. These problems took time to resolve and slowed down my progress during the later stages of the project.

This showed that I need more experience with deployment environments. Going forward, I will spend more time practicing with environment variables, database access settings, and debugging deployment issues more efficiently.

---

## 3. I need to plan better for working with external systems

During integration, I had to make adjustments to handle responses from multiple APIs and ensure everything worked consistently. Although everything worked in the end, some of these adjustments could have been anticipated earlier.

In the future, I will plan integration details ahead of time, such as response structure and error handling, to reduce the need for changes later and make the process smoother.
